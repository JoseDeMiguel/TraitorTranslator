The Amazon Rainforest, often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth," is one of the largest and most biodiverse ecosystems on the planet. It spans across nine countries in South America, including Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The forest covers approximately 2.1 million square miles and is home to an astonishing variety of plants and animals. Many of the species that inhabit the Amazon are still unknown to science. The rainforest plays a critical role in regulating the Earth's climate, absorbing carbon dioxide, and producing oxygen. However, deforestation has become a major problem, as large areas of the forest are cleared for agriculture, logging, and mining. This deforestation contributes to climate change by releasing large amounts of carbon into the atmosphere. Additionally, the loss of the rainforest threatens countless species and disrupts the livelihoods of indigenous communities who rely on the forest for food, shelter, and medicine. Efforts to protect the Amazon have been ongoing, but the challenges remain significant due to economic pressures and political factors. It is crucial for the global community to work together to preserve this vital ecosystem.

Why is the Amazon Rainforest called the "lungs of the Earth"?
a) Because it helps to produce oxygen
b) Because it provides shelter to animals
c) Because it is the largest forest in the world
Correct answer: a) Because it helps to produce oxygen

How many countries does the Amazon Rainforest span across?
a) Four

What is the main reason for deforestation in the Amazon?
a) Urbanization
b) Agriculture, logging, and mining

Correct answer: b) Agriculture, logging, and mining

Why is deforestation in the Amazon a global issue?

b) It contributes to climate change and species loss
